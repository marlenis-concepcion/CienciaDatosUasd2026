# FIA/UASD APA 7 Rules Summary

Source: `FIA_Normas APA 7ma edicion.pdf`, Centro de Escritura Javeriano, Pontificia Universidad Javeriana Cali, julio de 2020. It summarizes APA Publication Manual 7th edition and includes Spanish-language adaptations.

## General Format

- Paper: letter size, 21.59 cm x 27.94 cm.
- Margins: 2.54 cm on all sides.
- Accepted fonts:
  - Times New Roman 12
  - Georgia 11
  - Computer Modern 10
  - Calibri 11
  - Arial 11
  - Lucida Sans Unicode 10
- Alignment: left, not justified.
- Paragraph indentation: first line 1.27 cm.
- References: hanging indent 1.27 cm.
- Page numbering: upper-right corner, Arabic numerals.
- Spacing: double-spaced; no extra space between paragraphs.

## Order of Elements

Suggested order:

1. Página de presentación
2. Resumen
3. Contenido/cuerpo del texto
4. Referencias
5. Notas al pie
6. Tablas
7. Figuras
8. Anexos

For student title pages, include: title, author, affiliation, course, professor, date, and page number. Institutions or teachers may add requirements.

## Abstract and Keywords

- Title `Resumen` centered and bold.
- Next line aligned left with no indentation.
- Usually up to 250 words unless the publication or assignment states otherwise.
- Keywords are normally used in scientific articles.
- Format: `Palabras clave: palabra 1, palabra 2, palabra 3`
- Keywords separated by commas and lowercase except proper nouns.

## Body

- Structure depends on genre. Scientific articles often use IMRD: Introducción, Método, Resultados, Discusión.
- Use citations to establish dialogue with sources.

## Footnotes

- Use brief notes only for additional or complementary information.
- Avoid excessive, irrelevant, or distracting information.
- Number footnotes with Arabic superscript numerals.

## Annexes

- Annexes help readers understand, evaluate, or replicate the argument or study.
- Label with letters: Anexo A, Anexo B, Anexo C.
- Mention annexes explicitly in the text, e.g. `(ver Anexo A)`.
- Each annex starts on a separate page after references.
- Each annex has a label and a brief descriptive title.

## Headings

- Level 1: centered, bold. Text starts in a new paragraph.
- Level 2: left-aligned, bold. Text starts in a new paragraph.
- Level 3: left-aligned, bold and italic. Text starts in a new paragraph.
- Level 4: indented paragraph heading, bold, ending with a period. Text starts on same line.
- Level 5: indented paragraph heading, bold and italic, ending with a period. Text starts on same line.

## Tables and Figures

Tables and figures must be useful, clear, explicitly mentioned in the text, numbered consecutively in Arabic numerals, and credited when adapted or copied.

Tables:

- Label and number in bold: `Tabla 1`.
- Short descriptive title in italic.
- Content uses horizontal lines.
- Table body may use 1.0, 1.5, or 2.0 spacing.
- Notes use the same font and size as the paper.
- If multiple notes exist, order: general, specific, probability.

Figures:

- Any non-table visual: chart, picture, drawing, illustration, etc.
- Label and number in bold: `Figura 1`.
- Short descriptive title in italic.
- Figure image/content should be high quality.
- Text inside figure may use sans serif 8-14 pt.
- Notes use same font and size as the paper.

Checklist:

- Relevant and clearer than prose.
- Adequate publication format.
- High-resolution file/format.
- Numbered consecutively.
- Mentioned explicitly in text.
- Short descriptive italic title.
- Clear labels and details.
- Abbreviations and special symbols explained.
- Relevant notes.
- Credits and permissions when needed.

## Citations

APA uses author-date citation.

General guidance:

- Prefer primary sources.
- Use reliable sources.
- Cite specific data, definitions, and claims.
- Avoid excessive direct quotes; prefer paraphrase.
- Paraphrase by understanding and rewriting, not replacing words with synonyms.
- Ensure every citation has a matching reference and every reference is cited.

Narrative citation:

- Author is part of sentence; year in parentheses.
- Example: `Quintero (2020) plantea que...`

Parenthetical citation:

- Author and year in parentheses.
- Example: `(Quintero, 2020)`.

Direct quotations:

- Must include author, year, and page.
- If one page, use `p.`; multiple pages use `pp.`.
- If no page, use paragraph number `párr.`, section heading, or timestamp for audiovisual material.

Short quotation:

- Under 40 words.
- Integrated in paragraph with quotation marks.
- Not italicized.

Long quotation:

- 40 words or more.
- Separate block.
- Left indent 1.27 cm.
- No quotation marks.
- Same font size and spacing.
- Period goes before parenthetical citation.

Paraphrase:

- Does not require page number, but page or paragraph can be useful for precise ideas.

Author count:

- One author: `López González (2019)` or `(López González, 2019)`.
- Two authors: `Rodríguez y Sánchez (2013)` or `(Rodríguez y Sánchez, 2013)`.
- Three or more authors: `Barton et al. (2016)` or `(Barton et al., 2016)` from first citation.
- Corporate author with abbreviation:
  - First narrative: `Organización Mundial de la Salud (OMS, 2015)`.
  - First parenthetical: `(Organización Mundial de la Salud [OMS], 2015)`.
  - Later: `OMS (2015)` or `(OMS, 2015)`.
- Corporate author without abbreviation: always full name.

Special citation situations:

- Multiple works in same parentheses: alphabetize by reference order and separate with semicolons.
- Same author, same year: `2019a`, `2019b`.
- Different authors with same last name: add initials.
- Same author, different years: order from oldest to newest, separated by semicolon.
- Translated/reissued works: use slash, e.g. `(Piaget, 1966/2000)`.
- No author: cite first words of title and year. Article/chapter/web page titles use quotation marks.
- Anonymous author: `(Anónimo, 2020)`.
- No date: `(s.f.)`.
- No page: use `párr.`, section heading, or timestamp.
- Secondary citation: `Penrose (como se citó en Hawking, 2010)`. Use sparingly.
- Personal communications: cite in text with initials, surname, exact date; omit from references.

## References

- Use `Referencias`, not `Bibliografía`, for APA papers.
- Include only sources explicitly cited.
- All cited sources must appear in references.
- All reference entries must be cited in the text.
- Title `Referencias` centered and bold.
- Alphabetical order by first author's surname.
- Double spacing.
- Hanging indent.

Reference elements:

1. Author: Who is responsible?
2. Date: When was it published?
3. Title: What is it called?
4. Source: Where can it be found?

Author rules:

- One author: `Apellido, A.`
- Two to twenty authors: list all authors.
- More than twenty authors: list first 19, ellipsis, final author.
- No author: start with title.

Common formats:

- Book: `Apellido, A. A. (Año). Título en cursiva. Editorial.`
- Book edition: `Apellido, A. A. (Año). Título en cursiva (2.ª ed.). Editorial.`
- Edited book: `Apellido, A. A. (Ed.). (Año). Título en cursiva. Editorial. URL`
- Electronic book: `Apellido, A. A. (Año). Título en cursiva. Editorial. URL`
- Translated book: include translator and original publication year.
- Book chapter: `Apellido, A. A. y Apellido, B. B. (Año). Título del capítulo. En A. A. Apellido (Ed.), Título del libro (pp. xx-xx). Editorial.`
- Journal article with DOI: `Apellido, A. A. (Año). Título del artículo. Nombre de la revista, volumen(número), pp-pp. https://doi.org/...`
- Online article: same format plus URL if no DOI.
- Newspaper: `Apellido, A. A. (Año, día de mes). Título del artículo. Nombre del periódico. URL`
- Government report: `Organización. (Año). Título del informe (Número de publicación). URL`
- Conference: `Apellido, A. (Año, día de mes). Título [tipo de contribución]. Nombre del congreso, Ciudad, País. URL`
- Thesis: `Apellido, A. (Año). Título [Tesis de maestría/doctoral, Universidad]. Repositorio. URL`
- Web page: `Autor. (Año, día de mes). Título. Nombre del sitio web. URL`
- Video: `Apellido, A. (Fecha). Nombre del video [Video]. Fuente. URL`
- Podcast: `Apellido, A. (Fecha). Título [Podcast]. URL`
- Social media: author/handle, date, first 20 words or description, format, site, URL.
- Laws: `Nombre de la ley. (Fecha). Fuente. Número de sección o artículo. URL`

Electronic material:

- Do not write `Recuperado de`.
- Do not write `DOI:` before DOI links.
- If no date, use `(s.f.)`.

## Spanish Adaptations

- Use `y` in narrative and parenthetical citations instead of `and` or `&`.
- In Spanish references, use `y` before the final author without a preceding comma.
- Use `&` only for references in English or other languages if preserving source language convention.
- Use sentence-style capitalization in Spanish titles.
- Format dates as `Año, día de mes`.
- Use lowercase month names.
- Use Spanish ordinal edition format: `(3.ª ed.)`.
